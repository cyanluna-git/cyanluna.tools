4. Definition of MVP / Pilot

For this project, MVP at this stage does not mean a broad feature-complete platform. It means the minimum stable level required to operate a meaningful pilot.

Pilot acceptance criteria

A. Manual operation baseline

  • An ME engineer must be able to complete one full loop of a 100% manual test independently
  • This is the baseline condition for pilot operation, with continuous improvement expected afterward

B. Automation demonstration baseline

The Pump Automation Test should be able to demonstrate:

  • Automatic start / stop of the pump
  • Acquisition of relevant sensor data
  • Pass / fail judgment based on collected values

This automation does not need to be perfect at the pilot stage. A meaningful and executable demonstration is sufficient.

Items that are not the priority for MVP

  • Full enterprise-level process formalization
  • Detailed long-cycle forecasting for every micro-feature
  • Heavy administrative overhead for very small implementation slices

5. Current Operating Model

The project is currently running in a very short feedback loop between design and implementation.

Operating characteristics

  • Gerald and Bipin are directly planning and implementing inside short feedback cycles
  • Small functional slices are handled directly as Kanban cards rather than expanded into full stories
  • The effective detailed planning horizon is typically 1-3 days
  • For this phase, milestone-based alignment plus short-horizon execution control is more suitable than a detailed two-week planning model

This is not a lack of planning. It is planning at the level that best fits the current product-shaping phase.

7. What I Need from the PM

At this stage, the most valuable PM contribution is not fine-grained Scrum administration on small implementation items. The higher-value role is external alignment, risk management, and structured support around execution gaps.

Expected PM support

  • Maintain stakeholder alignment on pilot scope, priorities, and tradeoffs
  • Manage schedule tracking
  • Support Scrum ceremonies where useful
  • Identify requirement gaps or blind spots that Gerald may not have explicitly surfaced yet
  • Convert external asks into decision-ready discussions
  • Track dependencies, risks, and follow-up actions
  • Maintain milestone-level visibility

Recommended practical model

  • Operate day-to-day execution primarily through Kanban
  • Use PM effort primarily for milestones, risks, dependencies, and gap management rather than micro-task control
  • Review status and risks weekly
  • Re-baseline scope quickly when needed

8. Reporting Needed

What is needed now is not a large reporting package, but a small set of reports that support decisions.

Essential reports

  • Pilot Readiness Report
    • Current milestone
    • Remaining critical scope
    • Top risks
    • Blockers affecting the April pilot
  • Committed vs. Delivered Report
    • Measured at the theme or milestone level, not at the micro-story level
    • What was intended in the last review window
    • What was actually delivered
    • What changed and why
  • Risk / Dependency Log
    • Schedule risks
    • External dependencies
    • Open decisions
    • Owners and target dates
  • Decision Log
    • Key product or delivery decisions
    • Why the decision was made
    • Impact on timeline, scope, or architecture
  • Gap / Requirement Watchlist
    • Requirements that may still be missing from the current plan or implementation
    • Operational gaps that must be checked before pilot

Lower-value reports for the current phase

  • Detailed sprint burndown focused on very small items
  • Story-point reporting for micro-slices of work
  • Formal status artifacts that duplicate the Kanban board without improving decisions

11. Key Alignment Message

The project is not under-planned. It is being planned at the level appropriate for the current phase.

The following must be explicit:

  • Pilot target and acceptance criteria
  • Milestone-level roadmap
  • Key risks and external dependencies
  • Clear status visibility for stakeholders

At the same time, forcing every small implementation step into a long-cycle process would not match the current delivery speed.
